Below are the three essential tools emphasized by Gartner this time. Develop an Incident Response Plan
A general plan for responding to cyber incidents should include the following:
- Map out the response process. The incident response plan should specify detailed steps to follow in an orderly manner when an incident occurs. The incident owner (or a similar role) should ensure that all steps are fully implemented and track progress and communication on a rolling basis. - Define incident severity levels. All security incidents must be classified and assigned a severity level. This helps guide incident escalation, clarify service level agreements, and inform stakeholders of the potential or actual impact on the organization in other ways. Severity levels also determine who should be notified, the escalation path, and which playbooks to use. - Clarify responsibilities. Effective incident response requires teamwork. The RACI model can clearly define all roles and corresponding responsibilities related to incident response across the entire organization.
