In this article, we start from Gartner's definition of threat intelligence and explore the several components it involves: context, indicators, and actionable advice. Data, Information, and Intelligence
The threat intelligence provider Recorded Future once wrote an article to analyze the differences between data, information, and intelligence. If interested, you can read it by visiting the link in the reference resources. Compared to many articles that borrow heavily from military and business fields, making threat intelligence seem obscure, Recorded Future's article is more grounded in cybersecurity. In my opinion, the mainstream application scenarios of threat intelligence combined with SOC/SIEM are far from comparable to military intelligence. First, we need to thoroughly collect and clean up multi-dimensional data. Only after completing these tedious and exhausting tasks and building the superstructure on top of them can we have a truly reliable foundation. Among the concepts of data, information, and intelligence, the definition of data is relatively clear, which can be understood as a record of a large number of independent objective facts. Information and intelligence, on the other hand, are different levels of conclusions obtained based on the processing of data. 没有人的预算足够支付所有可能的风险缓解措施。 决策者必须选择哪些措施可能以最低的成本产生最大的影响。 他们已经为多层次的网络安全堆栈提供了资金,以实现纵深防御。 人员成本通常远高于工具成本。 所有组织至少在每个端点上都有一个基于软件的防御(例如,传统的防病毒软件、端点保护平台)。 其他层次部署在端点之前(即边界)、周围(例如,入侵检测、实体用户行为分析等)和之后(SIEM、XDR等)。 来自端点防御的警报也应被视为涉及端点防御之后的资源,因为直到分析师调查后才会阻止或终止任何事情。 而且,不要忘记补救和其他事件后的成本。 完美的预防性端点保护将大大减少“周围”和“之后”层的人员工作量。 相反,更多的端点保护失败会增加“周围”和“之后”的成本。 这也增加了风险,因为当人员追查大量相对低影响的事件时,他们无法处理高影响的事件。 警报疲劳也会降低人员效率,从而增加风险。 决策者总是在众多潜在的网络安全投资中挣扎选择。
