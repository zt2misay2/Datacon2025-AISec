The full name of GDPR is the General Data Protection Regulation. This legislation was drafted in January 2012, and after four years of discussion and negotiation, the EU officially passed this regulation and announced a trial run in April 2016. It came into full effect on May 25, 2018. Due to the GDPR stipulation that companies can face fines of up to 4% of their global turnover for violations, it has been dubbed the "strictest data protection regulation in history." Previously, major data breach incidents in 2017 and the recent scandal involving Facebook and Cambridge Analytica's collection of user data were all secretly relieved that they did not encounter the official implementation of GDPR; otherwise, their situations would have been even more severe.
Privacy protection and data security have become important components of corporate security capability building. However, enhancing data privacy protection in a complex IT system environment poses a significant challenge to traditional data loss prevention technologies and products, adding a heavy burden to corporate data management. Large enterprises, with their complex internal network environments and data storage architectures, require technical support across multiple stages such as data discovery, management, and classification for effective data privacy protection. Data collaboration and sharing among multiple departments necessitate the correlation and aggregation analysis of data to more reasonably identify potential privacy leak risks.
